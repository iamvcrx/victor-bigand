Musical creativity around textures, sound design and atmospheres

I studied classical violin from the age of 5 until I was 18. Then, I discovered the world of digital musical creation and never stopped evolving in this direction. Maybe one day these two directions will merge into one. For the moment, I work on creating instrumentals for artists as well as creating instrumental electronic music for myself. My work focuses on textures, sound design and energies, visiting a large range of musical genres from house music to breakbeat.

Electronic music production : Loustiks & vcrx

Since 2022, I produce instrumental electronic music in a group called Loustiks. We started working on techno tracks like « Thalia » or « Underwater » with a melodic inspiration (you can listen to samples below if you’re not on phone version of the website). Then, we tried visiting house music in a solar EP called « Gelati » with tracks like « Stracciatella » or « Attention, canicule ». We lastly experimented on sound textures and rhythms in our EP « Pulsions ». You can click here to access the artist page.

As a continuation of this work, I composed my first EP called « Introspection » under the name « vcrx ». This project of instrumental electronic music is the fruit of personal reflections. The lack of text makes it a simple invitation for the listeners to travel in their mind and reflect upon themselves.
To achieve this, I worked mainly on the texture of synthesized sounds, creating cinematic atmospheres based on pads (spacious and slow sounds), textures such as ocean sounds and film dialogue extracts, often mixed in such a way as to retain only the mood and not the meaning.
For the rythmic part, my main inspirations come from techno and breakbeat, two repetitive rhytmic styles revisited in a soft way while retaining their impact to recall an introspective journey. You can click here to listen to the project.

Sound design for dance

« La parole des vents » wich can translate as « The talk of winds » is a dance piece by the company "Les résilient.e.s" exploring the relationship with oneself, with others and with shocks through a 24-hour journey by 6 dancers immersed in nature. I am involved in the creation of the soundtrack for the show, which evokes the passing day. We used sounds of nature recorded during residencies and explored sound design techniques to make these nature sounds follow the narrative of the piece.

The creative work was based on an analysis of the various sound ambiences occuring in a day surrounded by nature. What different experiences can ambient sounds bring to our ears from morning 10am to evening 7pm ? We went on a residency to analyze and record these differences. The mixing phase also proved to be particularly creative, to translate the intentions of the narrative into the sounds of nature. We used reverberations on wind noises to convey cold and wide-open spaces, or distortions and analog noises on dry wind and sand sounds to evoke a sultry, stifling early afternoon.

Music creation at the intersection of AI and dance

The dancer Marie Bruand and the PhD student Sarah Nabi at IRCAM (STMS laboratory) started a project in 2023 that seeks to explore music and movement interactions by generating live music from body sensors on the dancer. To achieve this, the signals captured are used to trace a trajectory in the latent space of a variational autoencoder neural network (RAVE) that has learned to generate music.
For the time being, this method generates an experimental sound experience and is interesting from a substantive point of view, as the latent space is continuous and vast. So, in comparison to deterministic methods where the interaction between movement and music is perfectly controlled, this new method could allow live exploration and unique performances.

On this project, we explored transfer methods where the neural network receives a musical input and modifies it based on what it learned. The dancer act as a modifier, exploring the latent space and thus the network expressivity. I am in charge of the musical creations. What makes this project particularly interesting is composing under the constraints of the specificities of the neural network (only certain types of sounds can be well regenerated).

A first performance was given in Paris for « Nuit Blanche » in 2023 (see video below), and another one took place in march on the occasion of IRCAM Forum.
